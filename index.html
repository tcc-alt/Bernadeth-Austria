The Pearl of the Orient Seas (Philippines)

Philippines other term are Tawalisi, Las Islas de San Lazaro, Las Islas de Poniente, Las Islas Felipenas, and Las Islas Filipinas. Philippines is an archipelago in Southeast Asia consist of 7,641 islands, with three main geographial division from north to south: Luzon, Visayas, Mindanao. During the Spanish time Ferdinand Magellan, a Portuguese explorer named the Islands after King Phillip II of Spain, it was called Las Felipinas. Did you know that this small county has 175 languages, and every language has its origin, dialect, and history with this country. Despite of having various languages Tagalog became the National language of the Philippines in 1937.

Philippines Natural Resources

Philippines is rich in Natural Resources this tropical country is well known for producing different varieties of crop such as coconut, banana, pineapples, corn, sugarcane. The country is one of the world's most richly endowed mineral resources. It is riched in copper, gold, nickel, zinc and silver. Filipino is also talented and skilled in creating handicrafts some of it are Woodcarving, Embroidery, Basket Weaving, Beadwork, Pottery, Paper Mache, Shellcraft, Metalwork, Leathercraft.

Philippines as a safe and good tourist destination.

The Philippines is currently ranked as the #20 safest country to travel to. There are many places in the Philippines that are considered one of the safest and most enjoyable, and one of these is Davao. The safety index is on a scale of zero to 100, with 100 being the best according to tourists; Davao falls at 72.29. You can also enjoy some island-hopping and climbing. The other one is Bohol, which is considered one of the great places to travel for those with an adventurous spirit. The scenery of nature will never fail you. You can explore the Chocolate Hills and rainforests, hit the Danao Adventure Park, or go ziplining, diving, sky-riding, river tubing, boat riding, and much more. The Bohol crime index fell to just 7.14, with a moderate rise in crime over the last few years. Next is Baguio; this place is famous for being known as the City of Pines. It is particularly popular in the summer due to the unusually cooler weather. You can enjoy beautiful architecture, botanical gardens, the Philippine Military Academy, Mt. Pulag National Park, the Diplomat Hotel, and more. Baguio has a crime index of just over 33, putting it in the low crime rate category. Another safest place to visit in the Philippines is Dumaguete. You can take advantage of peace and serenity at a bird sanctuary, watch dolphins, or relax by the water. One of the famous places in Dumaguete is Pulangbato Falls, where you can also try their outdoor market full of exotic food. Dumaguete is considered to have a low crime rate, with the index being just below 40. In addition to this, Aklan is the safest and most adventurous place to visit. We all know that its captivating view will never fail. Boracay is located in Aklan. There are many tourists who have had unforgettable experiences here. The island has a considerable number of tourists day and night, and public places are often crowded, which discourages potential threats. Also, there are visible police and security personnel throughout the island, ensuring order and safety. Boracay boasts pristine white sand beaches and smaller islands, breathtaking sunsets, and fun water activities. As for the record, Boracay reached and accommodated 1,759,592 tourists in 2022. The Philippines never fails to provide an awesome experience for every tourist that visits the country. There are many mesmerizing places and breathtaking views we cannot resist in the Philippines.
